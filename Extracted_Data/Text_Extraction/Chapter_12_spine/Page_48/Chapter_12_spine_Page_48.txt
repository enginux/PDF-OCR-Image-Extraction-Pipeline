Recurrent disc herniation
Sagittal T2-weighted MRI
Axial post-contrast T1-weighted MRI
Recurrent disc herniation: MRI demonstrates a heterogeneous signal intensity, predominantly T2 hyperintense
disc protrusion at L5-S1 (arrows). Post-contrast MRI confirms that this protrusion enhances peripherally
(arrows), confirming recurrent disc herniation rather than scar or granulation tissue.
In a patient with recurrent pain after decompressive surgery, an intravenous contrast-
enhanced MRI can help differentiate between recurrent disc herniation (which may be
treated surgically) versus scar and granulation tissue.
A recurrent disc would not enhance, or would enhance only peripherally.
Scar tissue and granulation tissue usually enhance homogeneously.
