THORACOLUMBAR SPINE TRAUMA
Overview of thoracolumbar trauma
The thoracolumbar injury classification and severity score (TLICS) is similar to SLICS for the
cervical (subaxial) spine, and aids in determination of optimal treatment.
The TLICS provides a numerical score for injury severity based on the same three
components of SLICS: injury morphology, integrity of the posterior ligamentous complex
(PLC), and neurological status.
The PLC is a primary stabilizer of the posterior spinal column, and knowledge of its anatomy
is helpful to recognize potentially unstable injuries. Indirect imaging findings of PLC injury
include widening of the interspinous distance, widening of the facet joints, spinous process
fracture, and subluxation or dislocation of a vertebral body.

Patients with a rigid spine - due to diffusion idiopathic skeletal hyperostosis (DISH),
ankylosing spondylitis, degenerative change, or surgical fusion, are more vulnerable to
fracture given altered biomechanics.
CT should be performed in a patient with rigid spine and back pain after even minor trauma.
Hyperextension is the most common injury mechanism.
