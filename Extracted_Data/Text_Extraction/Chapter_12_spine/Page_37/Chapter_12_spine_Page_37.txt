Abnormal pedicles
If one pedicle is abnormal and the other pedicle is normal, a lesion may be present.
Rarely, if one pedicle is sclerotic and the other pedicle is lucent, there may be congenital absence of the
pedicle (on the lucent side) with contralateral stress-related changes.
If one pedicle is sclerotic and the other pedicle is normal, then a sclerotic lesion may
be present, such as metastasis or osteoid osteoma. However, this pattern of a unilateral
sclerotic pedicle can also be seen in the setting of unilateral spondylolysis with reactive
changes on the opposite side causing sclerosis.
