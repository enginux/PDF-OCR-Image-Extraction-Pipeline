GENERAL RADIOGRAPHY
X-ray generator
X-ray photons are generated when high-energy electrons hit a target at the
anode side of the circuit. For general radiography and CT, the target is made of
tungsten (atomic symbol W).
99% of the electrons' kinetic energy is converted to heat, and 1% is converted to X-rays.
90% of X-rays are produced from bremsstrahlung (nuclear field interaction).
The maximum keV (energy) of the X-ray spectrum is the kV of the generator. The average
