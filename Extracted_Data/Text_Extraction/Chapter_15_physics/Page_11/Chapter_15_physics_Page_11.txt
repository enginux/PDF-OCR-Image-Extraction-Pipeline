Beam quality and half
A "high quality" beam has low-energy photons filtered out.
value layer (HVI)
The half-value layer (HVL) is a measurement of the beam quality and is the
thickness of material that attenuates 50% of the incident energy.
HVL is dependent on photon energy. HVL T with T photon energy.
Aluminum (Al) is the standard material for measuring HVL.
Typical HVL for mammography is 0.3 mm Al.
Typical HVL for radiography is 3 mm Al.
State regulations require beam quality: HVL >2.5 mm Al at 80 kVp.
Typical HVL for CT is 8-9 mm Al.
