Tetralogy of Fallot (TOF) is the most
common cyanotic heart disease of
children and adults

The degree of cyanosis depends on the degree of right ventricular outflow tract obstruction.
TOF is associated with DiGeorge syndrome (absent thymus and parathyroids, causing
hypocalcemia), VACTERL, and trisomy 21. 25% of cases of TOF have a right-sided aortic arch.
Classic radiographic findings of TOF are a boot-shaped heart (cardiac apex uplifted by RV
hypertrophy, although the heart is normal in size) and decreased pulmonary vascularity.
Surgical repair involves closure of VSD and opening of right ventricular outflow tract
obstruction, which is usually performed in infancy.
