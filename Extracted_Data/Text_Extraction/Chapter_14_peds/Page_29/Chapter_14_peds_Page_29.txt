Atrioventricular septal defect (AVSD), also known as common AV canal or endocardial
cushion defect (ECD), encompasses a spectrum of partial to complete defects involving
ostium primum ASD, VSD, and mitral or tricuspid anomalies. AVSD is strongly associated
with trisomy 21.
The primitive endocardial cushion is responsible for the formation of several structures in
fetal development, including:
Posterior and membranous ventricular septum.
Anterior leaflet of the mitral valve.
Septal leaflet of the tricuspid valve.
Responsible for closure of the ostium primum.
