Congenital anomalies of the SVC
Normally, the embryologic left anterior cardinal vein regresses and the right anterior
cardinal vein develops into the SVC.
Left-sided SVC is due to persistence of the embryological left anterior cardinal vein and
regression of the right anterior cardinal vein. It is the most common congenital venous
anomaly in the chest.
The left SVC usually drains directly into the coronary sinus - right atrium.
Rarely, the left SVC drains directly into the left atrium, causing a right to left shunt.
Left-sided SVC is weakly associated with congenital heart disease (CHD). Left SVC is an
incidental finding in 0.5% of the population but is seen in 4% of patients with CHD.
. A duplicated SVC is due to persistence of both the right and left anterior cardinal veins.
Duplicated SVC also carries an association with CHD.
