Parts of the axillary artery
The first portion runs from the lateral edge of the first rib to the upper border of the pectoralis minor
muscle and branches inferiorly into the superior thoracic artery.
The second portion runs behind the pectoralis minor muscle and branches superolaterally into the
thoracoacromial trunk andi inferiorly into the lateral thoracic artery.
The third portion runs from the lateral border of the pectoralis minor muscle to the teres major muscle,
branching into the subscapular artery and the anterior and posterior circumflex humeral arteries.

Brachial artery
The brachial artery begins at the lateral edge of the teres major muscle. The branches
include the deep brachial artery, the superior ulnar collateral artery, and the inferior ulnar
collateral artery.
The radial nerve parallels the deep brachial artery.
