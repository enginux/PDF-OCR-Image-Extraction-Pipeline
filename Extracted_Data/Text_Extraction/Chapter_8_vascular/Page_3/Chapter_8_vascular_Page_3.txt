A common origin of the brachiocephalic artery and left common carotid artery is seen in
13% of patients (more commonly in black patients) and is often incorrectly referred to as a
"bovine aortic arch." The term "bovine aortic arch" is a misnomer and is not the preferred
description of this anomaly, as a true bovine arch in cattle features a single great vessel
arising from the aortic arch.

An aberrant right subclavian is seen in 1% of patients. The right subclavian artery arises
directly from the aortic arch distal to the left subclavian and loops behind the esophagus on
its way into the right arm.
