Chondrosarcoma, arising from malignant degeneration of enchondroma: Radiographs and MRI in the same
patient as above, obtained 11 years later, demonstrate a subtle lytic lesion of the humeral head (arrows).
MRi imaging shows a large T1 hypointense, T2 hyperintense, enhancing mass in the proximal humerus with
cortical thinning anteriorly (red arrow). The mass is markedly enlarged since 11 years prior, and involves nearly
the entire marrow cavity. Note the punctate mineralizations (blue arrows), which have not increased since 11
years.
Please note that malignant transformation of an isolated enchondroma is extremely rare, and it is not typical
clinical practice to follow benign-appearing enchondromas. indefinitely.
