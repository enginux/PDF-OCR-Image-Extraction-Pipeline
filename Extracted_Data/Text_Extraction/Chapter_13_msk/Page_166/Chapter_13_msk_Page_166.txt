The scaphoid is the most frequently injured carpal bone, commonly due to a fall on
outstretched hand. Prompt identification is critical, to allow optimal treatment decision-
making (casting versus surgical repair); however, scaphoid fractures are often occult on
radiography.
MRI plays an important role in detection of suspected occult scaphoid fracture.

Blood supply to scaphoid is interosseous, from distal to proximal in a retrograde fashion. The
proximal pole of the scaphoid has a tenuous blood supply and proximal fractures have the
highest risk of avascular necrosis.
