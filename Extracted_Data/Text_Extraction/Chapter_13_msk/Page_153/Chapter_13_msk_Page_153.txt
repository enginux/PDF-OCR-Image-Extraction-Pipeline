Posterior instability
Posterior instability is uncommon, accounting for less than 5% of instability.
The injuries associated with posterior instability are highly variable and include several
posterior variations of anterior lesions such as posterior HAGL (PHAGL), reverse Bankart
(posterior glenoid fracture), and reverse Hll-Sachs/trough sign.
Tears of the infraspinatus and teres minor are associated with posterior instability, as these
rotator cuff muscles insert on the posterior aspect of the greater tuberosity.
Hypoplastic posterior glenoid may be a congenital cause of posterior instability.
The Bennett lesion, also known as thrower's exostosis, is extra-articular posterior ossification
associated with posterior labral injury. The Bennett lesion is often seen in baseball pitchers
and is thought to be the result of avulsion of the posterior band of the IGHL. Ossification can
be confirmed with an axillary shoulder radiograph.
