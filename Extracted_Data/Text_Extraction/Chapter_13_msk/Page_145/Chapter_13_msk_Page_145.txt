Adhesive capsulitis, clinically known as frozen shoulder, is thickening and contraction of the
glenohumeral joint capsule due to inflammation of the joint capsule and synovium. This
results in pain and loss of range of motion.
Adhesive capsulitis is primarily a clinical diagnosis, but MRI findings include thickening,
edema, and enhancement of the joint capsule (inferior glenohumeral ligament >4 mm),
effacement of fat in the rotator interval, and thickening of the coracohumeral ligament.
Radiographs are often normal but are helpful to exclude other causes of pain, such as
glenohumeral osteoarthritis or calcific tendinopathy.
