with bone marrow edema in the lateral femoral condyle and the posterolateral tibial plateau
is highly suggestive of ACL injury. Secondary buckling of the posterior collateral ligament
(PCL) may also be seen due to anterior displacement of the tibia (positive drawer. sign).

A clue to ACL injury on radiography is a Segond fracture, which is an avulsion fracture of
the lateral tibia plateau. Segond fracture is associated with both ACL injury and iliotibial (IT)
band injury. The IT band inserts on Gerdy's tubercle of the tibia.
The Segond fracture is thought to represent detachment of the lateral capsular ligament.
